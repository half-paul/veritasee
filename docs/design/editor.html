By word count, Lovelace's notes are closer to 2.5× the length of Menabrea's paper, not 3×. Menabrea's Notions sur la machine analytique runs roughly 5,400 words; Lovelace's Notes A–G together total approximately 13,000 words [1].

The 3× figure appears repeatedly in secondary literature but appears to originate from a single 1979 paraphrase that has been re-cited without re-measurement [2]. The British Library's manuscript catalogue gives the original wordcount estimates used here.
