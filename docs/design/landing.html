§ Editorial principle

Humans
hold the pen.
AI fetches
the evidence.

Every published correction on Veritasee is authored by a verified human. AI tools can compile supporting and contradicting evidence — they stage findings into the editor — but a contributor must review, edit, and explicitly confirm review before submission is enabled.

The Verity Score is a rationale, not a verdict: 0–100% on a per-section basis, with a written justification of forty characters minimum. Sections drift. Sources change. We version, we re-anchor, and we warn when the ground moves.

Governance scales as the contributor base matures: a single moderator queue at launch, reputation-weighted auto-approval at v1.2, topic-scoped review thereafter. The pressure release is built in.

Wikipedia · en · Treaty of Tordesillas

Treaty of Tordesillas

SOURCE-REV · a4f2e1c… FETCHED · 2026·05·18 08:14 UTC 3 OVERRIDES · 1 PENDING

The Treaty of Tordesillas, signed on 7 June 14941, divided the newly discovered lands outside Europe between the Portuguese Empire and the Crown of Castile. The dividing line lay halfway between Cape Verde and the islands entered by Columbus on his first voyage.

The lands east of the line would belong to Portugal and lands west to Castile. The treaty was negotiated at Tordesillas in modern-day Spain2, and it was signed by representatives of both monarchies.

The other side of the world was divided a few decades later by the Treaty of Zaragoza, signed in 1529, which specified the antimeridian to the line of demarcation specified in the Treaty of Tordesillas. The Portuguese and Spanish were able to settle their imperial competition without resorting to peaceful negotiation3.
