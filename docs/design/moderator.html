Wikipedia · en › Ada Lovelace › § Work on the Analytical Engine · ¶ 3 · Correction-ID c_3kx8w2

A claim of 3× length is closer to 2.5×.

Submitted · 2h 41m ago Topic · History Topic · Technology Snapshot · a4f2e1c
28 PCT

Source paragraph rev a4f2e1c · 2026·05·15

Lovelace's notes are approximately three times the length of Menabrea's original paper, and they include detailed criticism of the engine's capability. Note G is the basis for her being credited as the first computer programmer — though that claim is itself contested.

Proposed correction markdown · 187 w

By word count, Lovelace's notes are closer to 2.5× the length of Menabrea's paper, not 3×. Menabrea's Notions sur la machine analytique runs roughly 5,400 words; Lovelace's Notes A–G together total approximately 13,000 words [1].

The 3× figure appears repeatedly in secondary literature but appears to originate from a single 1979 paraphrase that has been re-cited without re-measurement [2]. The British Library's manuscript catalogue gives the original wordcount estimates used here.

28 Verity · proposed
“Original section is mostly accurate but the 3× wordcount figure is sloppy and frequently re-cited without re-measurement. Adjusting to 2.5× brings the claim in line with primary manuscript count.”
